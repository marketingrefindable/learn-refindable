I

What is Finnita + HubSpot

About Finnita

Finnita is an HR Tech company that helps employees reduce their student loan debt. Finnita acts as an intermediary between employees and loan brokers/servicers (like Gotzoom) who provide repayment plans. Employers contract Finnita as a workplace benefit — their employees then get access to plans that can reduce their monthly loan payments significantly.

1How the Service Works
  • Employers sign up with Finnita to offer student loan benefits to their employees
  • Employees register through the Finnita app or landing page (register.finnita.com)
  • Finnita connects the employee with a broker/servicer (e.g., Gotzoom) who offers a repayment plan
  • The employee chooses a plan with help from the call center or the app
  • Finnita charges a one-time Certification Fee (~$407) and a recurring Monthly Fee (~$32.95)
  • The employee's loan payments are reduced — average savings of 63% monthly
2What is HubSpot?

HubSpot is our CRM (Customer Relationship Management) platform. It's where we track every contact, every deal, every subscription, and every communication. Think of it as the single source of truth for everything happening with Finnita's clients.

Your HubSpot URL: app.hubspot.com — log in with the credentials provided by your administrator.

As a call center agent, your primary job is to catch leads who start the process but don't finish. The app handles most enrollments automatically — you step in when someone drops off, needs help, or has questions. You are the safety net that ensures no potential client falls through the cracks.

3When You Connect: What to Cover

When you reach the contact, here's what you need to do on the call:

VI

Subscriptions & Invoices

🔴 CRITICAL SECTION — READ CAREFULLY

Creating a subscription is the most important action you'll perform. It triggers real charges to the client. Double-check every field before saving. An error here means the client gets charged incorrectly.

How Subscriptions Work

When a client is ready to enroll, you create 1 subscription in HubSpot that includes 2 products:

1Understanding the Subscription
ComponentWhatExample
Product 1: Certification FeeOne-time upfront paymentAthens ISD — Certification Fee: $407.00
Product 2: Monthly FeeRecurring monthly chargeAthens ISD — Monthly Fee: $32.95/month
Start DateWhen the first payment is dueThe date you create it, or a future date
Number of PaymentsHow many months the subscription runsVaries by plan chosen

When you create the subscription, HubSpot automatically generates invoices that are emailed to the client. The first invoice includes the Certification Fee. Subsequent invoices are for the Monthly Fee only.

[CONFIRMAR] Does the first invoice include BOTH the cert fee + first monthly fee, or just the cert fee alone? Also: does the monthly fee start in the same month or the following month?
2Step-by-Step: Creating a Subscription

From the deal record:

  • Step 1: Make sure all deal properties are filled (plan, loan data, income, etc.)
  • Step 2: In the right panel, find "Subscriptions" and click "+ Create Subscription"
  • Step 3: Set the subscription name — use format: [Employer Name] — [Contact Name]
  • Step 4: Add Product 1 — Search for the employer's Certification Fee product (e.g., "Athens ISD — Certification Fee")
  • Step 5: Add Product 2 — Search for the employer's Monthly Fee product (e.g., "Athens ISD — Monthly Fee")
  • Step 6: Set Billing Frequency: Monthly
  • Step 7: Set Start Date: The date the first payment should be charged
  • Step 8: Set Number of Payments: According to the plan chosen by the client
  • Step 9: Double-check everything — amounts, dates, products, contact email
  • Step 10: Click Create

What happens next: HubSpot automatically creates an invoice and sends it to the client's email. The client clicks the payment link in the email to pay.

Screenshot: Create Subscription dialog showing 2 products
Deal → Subscriptions → "+ Create" → Show the full form with both products added
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
  • Wrong employer product — Each employer has specific products. Search carefully. "Athens ISD — Monthly Fee" is NOT the same as "Harmony ISD — Monthly Fee".
  • Wrong amount — Most monthly fees are $32.95, but some employers have custom pricing ($29.95, $33.95). Always verify.
  • Wrong contact email — The invoice goes to the email on the subscription. Make sure it's correct.
  • Forgetting to associate — The subscription must be associated with both the deal and the contact.
3After Creating the Subscription
  • Confirm with the client on the phone: "You'll receive an email with your invoice shortly. Please check your inbox and spam folder."
  • Move the deal to "Paid" once the first payment is received
  • Continue moving through stages: Ready to Enroll → Submitted to Processing → Program Active
  • Update the contact's Subscription Status to "Active"

How Payments Flow

Payment Lifecycle
Subscription created→ Invoice auto-generated→ Sent via email→ Client clicks payment link→ Payment received ✅
1When a Payment Fails

If a monthly payment fails (e.g., client's card declines on March 5):

TimelineWhat happens
Day 0 (March 5)Payment fails. Invoice status → "Past Due". Payment Status → "Due"
Days 1-30Grace period. The client can still pay. HubSpot may retry automatically.
Day 30+ (April 5)If still unpaid: Deal automatically moves to "Suspended Client". Subscription Status → "Past Due".
